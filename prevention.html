Prevention

Learning how to prevent starts with learning the causes. From there, we can look into each specific cause and learn how to prevent it.

Poverty & Economic Inequality

Cause #1: Poverty & Economic Inequality

Poverty is a big cause of gun violence. Almost 25% of people in Philadelphia live in poverty, leading to hopelessness and desperation. Poverty often restricts individuals access to employment. This creates an open area for high stress and this could lead to desperation. Poverty also means a lack of resources and affordable healthcare. Without these resources, people could feel marginalized or disconnected from the rest of society, leading to social unrest and/or illegal activites.

1/2

Ways to Prevent:

Preventing poverty in Philadelphia requires more than one solution. Investing in education and training for work can help people get jobs and reduce the unemployment rate. Another way to prevent poverty is by implementing policies that promote affordable housing and healthcare access can alleviate financial burdens on low-income families. Furthermore, supporting small businesses and promoting economic development in underserved communities can create job opportunities and help stimulate local economies. Finally, there are already many foundations that are trying to help reduce poverty rates in Philly like:

The Promise,   United Way, and  SVP Philadlephia

Gang Activities & Drug Trade

Cause #2: Gang Activities & Drug Trade

Preventing gang violence and drug trafficking in Philadelphia necessitates targeted strategies. Investing in education and vocational training provides alternatives to illicit activities. Strengthening community policing fosters trust and disrupts gang operations. Implementing economic development initiatives in high-risk areas reduces gang influence and provides opportunities for at-risk youth. Supporting rehabilitation programs for gang members and enhancing collaboration between law enforcement and community organizations are crucial for breaking the cycle of violence.

1/2

Ways to prevent:

Targeted interventions addressing root causes are imperative to prevent Gang Violence and Drug Trafficking. Investing in education and job training programs provides alternatives to illegal activities. Strengthening community policing efforts disrupts gang operations and fosters trust. Implementing economic development initiatives in underserved areas reduces gang influence and enhances opportunities. Additionally, supporting rehabilitation programs for gang members and enhancing collaboration between law enforcement and community organizations are vital for breaking the cycle of violence and poverty.

Easy Access to Firearms

Cause #3: Easy Access to Firearms

Easy access to firearms in Philadelphia contributes significantly to the city's gun violence problem. Despite strict gun control laws, illegal firearms flow into the city through various channels, including straw purchases, theft, and trafficking from neighboring states with lenient regulations. The rapid increase of the number of guns increases the likelihood of violence and escalates conflicts, making the challenges faced by communities already grappling with socioeconomic disparities and other systemic issues even worse.

1/2

Ways to Prevent:

Preventing the ease of access to firearms in Philadelphia needs more than one solution. Strengthening background checks and licensing requirements can prevent illegal ownership of deadly firearms. Enhanced enforcement against illegal gun trafficking and unlicensed dealers is crucial. Implementing community buyback programs and promoting safe storage practices can reduce the availability of firearms. Additionally, public awareness campaigns on responsible gun ownership and advocacy for stricter federal regulations contribute to mitigating the flow of firearms into the city.
