Learn Where

Now that you know the numbers, we need to continue. Learning about Gun Violence and then being able to prevent it, isn't helpful without knowing where it happens the most. Instead of looking at Philadelphia as a whole, its better to look at it seperately. In these next few sections, you will deep dive into the three most populated sections of Philadelphia, be able to look at a map, and be able to learn where gun violence happens the most.

Overview of the Maps

In these next few sections, you will learn about the amounts of gun violence in North Philly, South Philly, and West Philly. These are areas where gun violence are most prominent, and have the densest population. In each section, you will be able to interact with a map, which have circles to indicate high to low areas of gun gun violence, but since it is so hard to get exact data points, these will be estimated. In each section, there is also a short summary explaining what the map shows and what we can learn from the datapoints given on the map.

North Philly

This is a map that outlines North Philly's gun violence. You can click on certain areas on the map for detailed statistics.

Red circles indicate high gun violence areas (100+ incidents)

Yellow circles indicate medium gun violence areas (50-99 incidents)

Green circles indicate low gun violence areas (below 20 incidents)

What can we learn from this?

The central part of North Philly is here most gun violence cases happen, there were about 550-600 incidents there. When going into northeast, we see a decrease, and by the top of the map, there are less incidents. In Northwest Philly (the left of the map), there are hardly any incidents, and only and max-estimate of 5 incidents.

South Philly

Red circles indicate high gun violence areas (100+ incidents)

South Philly in total is not a big area. Most of the gun violence is spread out so its hard to place more than one circle on the map. South Philly in general is a "high violence" area based on the categories that I created, but it's max-estimate is 130, nowhere near as high as North Philly.
